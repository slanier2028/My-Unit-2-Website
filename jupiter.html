Jupiter

The biggest and possibly the oldest planet in our solar system. It has a huge storm known as the Great Red Spot. This planet is one of the two gas giants in our solar system.

Facts

  • A day on Jupiter is 10 hours long which is shorter than a day on Earth, but a year on Jupiter takes 11.8 Earth years since its orbit is more far out in the solar system.

  • Jupiter has 101 officially recognized moons.

  • Jupiter is one of the four planets in our solar system that you wouldn't be able to stand on since Jupiter and the other giant planets (Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune) do not have a solid surface like the terrestrial planets.

  • This planet is twice as big as the other planets in our solar system combined.

  • Jupiter has rings, but it's very hard to see.

  • One of Jupiter's moons, Ganymede, is the biggest moon in the solar system. It's larger than Mercury! Ganymede is also the only moon thats known to have a magnetic field. Its magnetic field can cause auroras or bright ribbons of glowing gas.

  • Jupiter's gravity helps Earth by deflecting and capturing comets and asteroids that could be a threat to the inner solar system. However, its gravity could also slingshot objects that could lead it to head towards Earth. This planet's gravity could serve as an advantage and disadvantage for our planet.

  • Jupiter gets its name from the king of ancient Roman gods.
