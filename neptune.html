Neptune

The farthest planet in our solar system. It's one of the two ice giants and is mainly known for its deep blue color. Similar to Uranus, it's made up of water, ammonia, and methane.

Facts

  • Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system, with its supersonic winds reaching speeds over 1,200 mph. That's faster than the speed of sound!

  • Neptune is the 2nd coldest planet in the solar system with it's temperatures being about -330° F.

  • It being one of the more recent planet discoveries, it was discovered in 1846 by Urbain Le Verrier, John Couch Adams, and Johann Galle.

  • Neptune is the first planet in our solar system to be discovered by using mathematics to predict its location.

  • A day on Neptune is 16 hours, but it takes 165 Earth years to complete an orbit around the Sun. It has only completed an orbit in 2011 after being discovered (sorry to anyone born later than 2011).

  • September 23rd is Neptune discovery day!

  • Neptune has 16 moons and 6 rings that are very hard to see.

  • Just like the other 3 giant planets, Neptune doesn't have a solid surface, making it impossible to stand on it.

  • It is suspected that Neptune rains diamonds due its extreme temperatures and conditions.

  • Although Neptune is usually shown with a deep blue color (top right image), Neptune has recently been shown to be a very blue pale color instead which might look like the image on the bottom (unfortunately).

  • Neptune's moon, Triton, orbits Neptune in the opposite direction than the other moons. In fact, it is the only large moon in the solar system with a retrograde orbit. People suspect that it may have been a dwarf planet that was captured by Neptune's gravity millions of years ago.

  • Neptune has a spot similar to the one Jupiter has. Neptune's storm called the "Great Dark Spot" is large enough to contain the entire Earth.

  • Neptune was named after the Roman god of the sea.
