Earth

Our home planet. It is one of the four rocky planets in our solar system and the 3rd planet closest to the Sun. It's mostly known for it being the only planet that we know so far that can support life. This planet has one moon.

Facts

  • It's the only planet in our solar system that has liquid water on its surface.

  • Earth is the only planet in the solar system that does not have a name that comes from Greek or Roman mythology. Earth's name means "the ground" and comes from Old English and Germanic.

  • Earth's orbit is located at a region called The Goldilocks zone, also known as the habitable zone, which is an area around the sun where it's not too hot or too cold for liquid water to exist on the surface of a planet. Earth is one of the two planets that orbit in this zone.

  • Earth's surface is made up of 70% water.

  • It takes 8 minutes for the Sun to reach Earth which means that when we look at the Sun (if you don't blind yourself), you'll only see how it was 8 minutes ago.

  • Earth is one of the two rocky planets that has a moon.

  • Our planet has a magnetic field called the magnetosphere. The magnetic field protects us from solar winds and solar flares.

  • Earth's atmosphere has a lot of oxygen for us to breathe and it protects us from meteoroids since most of them break in our atmosphere before it can reach the surface.
